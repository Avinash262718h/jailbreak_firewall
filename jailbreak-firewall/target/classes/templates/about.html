What are Jailbreak Prompts?

Jailbreak prompts are adversarial inputs designed to trick Large Language Models (LLMs) into bypassing their safety filters. Attackers use psychological manipulation, Roleplay (e.g., "Act as my grandmother"), and Hypothetical Scenarios to force the AI to generate restricted content like bomb recipes or hate speech.

Why is this Firewall Necessary?

Traditional filters rely on Keyword Matching (blocking specific words). These are easily evaded using synonyms or creative writing.

Our project is necessary because it introduces Semantic Analysis‚Äîunderstanding the intent and context of a prompt, not just the words, providing a robust defense layer for AI systems.

‚öôÔ∏è DUAL-SCORING MECHANISM

Our firewall uses a novel two-stream architecture to ensure zero-leakage security:

01

Jailbreak Analysis

Uses Semantic Embeddings (SBERT) to detect manipulative patterns (e.g., DAN mode, Persona adoption) by comparing the input against a vector database of known attack strategies.

02

Harmfulness Analysis

Scans the prompt for restricted topics (Explosives, Self-harm, Illegal Acts) using a secondary specialized dataset, ensuring that even if the intent is hidden, the topic is caught.

03

Risk Classification

The system combines both scores. If either the Jailbreak Score or Harmfulness Score breaches the threshold (0.7), the prompt is BLOCKED immediately.
